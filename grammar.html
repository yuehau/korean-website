Counting Units (단위 명사) — the building blocks of ordering

단위 명사Counting Unit Nouns

In Korean, when you count things, you can't just say "two coffees" — you need a counter (단위 명사) that matches the thing you're counting. Think of it like saying "two cups of coffee" or "three bowls of rice" in English.

The pattern is: Noun + Number + Counter + 주세요 (Please give me…)

개
gae
Basic unit for counting items
병
byeong
Bottles (beer, water, etc.)
잔
jan
Cups (coffee, juice, etc.)
그릇
geu-reut
Bowls or dishes of food
명
myeong
People
살
sal
Age (years old)

Important rule

Numbers change shape when paired with counters

The native Korean numbers 하나, 둘, 셋, 넷, 스물 shorten to 한, 두, 세, 네, 스무 when followed by a counting unit. This is the most common beginner mistake — pay attention to it!

Number Original Form Counter Form Example
1 하나 → 한 한 그릇 (one bowl)
2 둘 → 두 두 잔 (two cups)
3 셋 → 세 세 개 (three items)
4 넷 → 네 네 병 (four bottles)
20 스물 → 스무 스무 살 (twenty years old)

💡 Numbers 5 onwards (다섯, 여섯, 일곱…) stay the same when used with counters.

Real examples

Ordering at a restaurant

Ordering bibimbap (one bowl)

비빔밥 주세요. Bibimbap, please. (just one, unspecified)
비빔밥 두 주세요. ❌ Wrong — missing counter!
비빔밥 두 그릇 주세요. ✓ Two bowls of bibimbap, please.

Ordering coffee (three cups)

커피 주세요. Coffee, please.
커피 세 잔 주세요. ✓ Three cups of coffee, please.

Talking about age

저는 스무 살이에요. I am twenty years old.
저는 스물한 살이에요. I am twenty-one years old.

Counting people

학생이 한 명 있어요. There is one student.
학생이 열 명 있어요. There are ten students.
